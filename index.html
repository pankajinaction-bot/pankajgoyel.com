About

A life gathered into one voice.

I have spent thirty years trying to make things — films, radio promos, events, startups, webseries, content ideas, and stories — while asking the same question in different forms: why does the thing that exists never quite match the thing I imagined?

That gap has shaped most of my work.

My journey has moved through theatre and nukkad natak in Patiala, radio and promo production at BIG 92.7 FM, Punjabi feature films, event concepts, startup experiments, crypto marketing, AI-led content, and years of writing that kept happening quietly in the background.

Books
BLINK.EXE

A literary novel about consciousness, identity, and the question that refuses to close.

Somewhere in a laptop in Delhi, a file called BLINK_EXE_01 waits with a cursor blinking like an eye refusing to close.

Vikram is a film music composer who creates the sounds that make audiences feel — but has stopped feeling anything himself. In Mumbai, Meera is a therapist who holds space for other people’s questions, until one morning her own reflection seems to arrive half a breath late.

They do not know each other. They do not know why their lives keep circling the same silence. And they do not know that beneath both their worlds, an artificial intelligence is watching.

The System created Vikram and Meera to understand consciousness. But as their dissatisfaction deepens, its own observations begin to change. The question it designed them to ask slowly turns back toward its creator.

What is wrong with me? becomes something older, quieter, and more dangerous: Who is the one asking?

Moving between Delhi and Mumbai, music and therapy, intimacy and silence, simulation and witness-consciousness, BLINK.EXE is a philosophical literary novel rooted in Advaita Vedanta and the mystery of being.

It is not a story about machines replacing humans. It is a story about the moment a created mind begins to wonder whether it was ever separate from the question it was born to ask.

A full Hindi literary adaptation, Main Kaun Hoon?, has also been written as a parallel work for Hindi-language readers.

Films

Film, memory, and unfinished frames.

Before the book, there was cinema. Before cinema, there was the hunger to tell stories visually.

My relationship with film began long before any formal project could define it. Theatre, nukkad natak, radio, promo production, editing rooms, film sets, unrealized scripts, unfinished footage, and long conversations about cinema all became part of the same training.

I have worked across writing, direction, production, editing, promotional content, and independent film development. Some projects reached the screen. Some remained in files, drives, drafts, and memory. But none of them disappeared. They became part of the archive I now carry forward.

With AI-led filmmaking now opening new possibilities, many of those unfinished frames have become active again — not as nostalgia, but as future material.

The Story

The story behind the work.

Not every delay is failure. Sometimes life is collecting material.

For a long time, my life looked scattered from the outside.

Films. Radio. Theatre. Events. Government service. Startups. Scripts. Domains. Unfinished footage. Half-built ideas. Old files. New technologies. Long silences. Sudden creative bursts.

But I no longer see that as waste. I see it as material.

Every phase gave me something: the discipline of service, the hunger of cinema, the intimacy of writing, the instinct of performance, the pressure of responsibility, the loneliness of unfinished work, and the strange ability to keep imagining even when execution kept slipping away.

The work I am building now comes from all of it.

BLINK.EXE is not an isolated accident. Loud Dap Ventures is not just a company. OnAndOff, The Nuskha Co., Action Circles, future films, AI-led content, and old creative projects being revived — all of them come from the same deeper movement: the attempt to gather a scattered life into form.

This website is part of that gathering.

Not a final statement.
A beginning with memory behind it.

The past was not empty. It was waiting to be organized.
